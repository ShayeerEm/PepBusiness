1
Reconstitute Your Vial
Using a fresh needle, draw the desired amount of bacteriostatic water and inject it slowly down the inside wall of the vial. Do not shake — gently swirl until clear.
2
Use a U100 Insulin Syringe
This calculator is calibrated for standard U100 (100 units = 1 mL) insulin syringes, which are the most common tool for peptide administration in a research setting.
3
Draw to the Unit Line
After reconstitution, insert the needle into the vial and draw back the plunger to the exact unit number shown in your results. Each small line on a U100 syringe = 1 unit = 0.01 mL.
4
Store Correctly
Refrigerate reconstituted peptides at 2–8°C. Use within 4–6 weeks. Never freeze a reconstituted vial. Keep unreconstituted vials in a cool, dark place.
